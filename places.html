Strointsy village

Stroentsy (Mold. Stroenţi, Ukrainian Stroїntsi) is a village in the Rybnitsa district of the unrecognized Transnistrian Moldavian Republic, 23 kilometers from the regional center - the city of Rybnitsa. Well-known tourist center of the north of Pridnestrovie. The exact time of the foundation of the village is unknown, but it is assumed that the village appeared at the beginning of the 18th century (presumably 1702). There are different opinions about the origin of the name of the village. According to the first, it comes from the word “build”, and according to the second version, the history of the village is connected with the colonization of the region after the acquisition of these lands by Prince Lubomirsky[3]. After the defeat in the Russian-Turkish war of 1711, the ally of Peter I, the Moldavian ruler Dmitry Cantemir, and more than four thousand Moldovans, fleeing the revenge of the Turks, left for Russia. In September 1711, leaving Transnistria and the right bank of the Dnieper, the tsarist government addressed the population with a proposal to move to the left bank

Chenromorsk City

The city of Chornomorsk is a large port and sea resort 30 km away southwest of Odesa. Until the middle of the XX century. there was a small fishing village Burlacha Balka and numerous farms. In 1950, on the shores of the Sukhoi estuary, the construction of a large military shipyard, a trading port and a workers' settlement began, which in 1952 was named Ilyichevsk in honor of the Bolshevik leader Lenin (Ilyich). However, the foundation date is considered to be 1973, when Ilyichevsk was included in the chilo of cities of regional subordination.
